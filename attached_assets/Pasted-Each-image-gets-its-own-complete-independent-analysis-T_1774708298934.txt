Each image gets its own complete independent analysis. Think of it like a teacher grading 10 homework papers — she doesn't grade them all together, she picks up paper 1, grades it fully, puts it down, picks up paper 2, grades it fully, and so on. Each paper gets its own score and feedback.

**About accuracy — it's the same accuracy as single image.** There's zero difference. The pipeline for image 1 doesn't know image 2 exists. Each one goes through the exact same steps: Azure Vision extracts signals → GPT-4.1 compares against rules JSON → returns structured result. Whether you upload 1 image or 10, each individual image gets the same quality analysis.

**The only thing batch adds is:**
- A summary on top (3 passed, 5 failed, 2 warnings)
- A CSV/PDF export button so the marketing team can download the full report
- Time savings — instead of uploading and waiting 10 separate times, do it once

It's not a different feature technically. It's just a loop + a nice frontend table. The AI pipeline is identical per image. That's actually the point — consistency regardless of whether you process 1 or 10.
